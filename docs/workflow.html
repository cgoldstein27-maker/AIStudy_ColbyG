Core workflow

This is the full study loop behind the app, from uploading notes to targeted re-review after quizzes.

Step 1: Build a note library

Add text or PDF notes and group them by class/topic.

Step 2: Create study material

Generate summaries and flashcards from the same notes.

Step 3: Practice with spacing

Review cards with confidence ratings that control scheduling.

Step 4: Close the loop

Use quiz outcomes to find weak topics and plan the next session.

Why this flow matters

Most study tools only handle one part of the process. This workflow keeps everything connected: your source notes, your practice material, your quiz performance, and your next review decision. That makes it easier to stay consistent across multiple school subjects.

How notes become practice

After notes are uploaded, the app breaks long text into manageable chunks. Those chunks power summary generation, flashcard prompts, and quiz question creation. The benefit is that each study mode is tied to the same content source, so terminology and definitions stay consistent with your class notes.

Review cycle and retention

Card reviews use an Again/Hard/Good/Easy model. Lower confidence brings cards back sooner, while higher confidence spaces them out. Over time, this shifts effort from easy material toward weaker areas, which is usually where most grade improvement comes from.

How quiz feedback drives the next session

Quiz results are not treated as a final score only. The app records weak topics so your next study session starts with what you missed. This creates a practical loop: study, test, identify weak points, then re-study with more focus instead of restarting from zero.
