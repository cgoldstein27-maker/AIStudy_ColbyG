Usability and design

This page explains the product choices that make the app easier to use during real school study sessions.

Low-friction setup

No account needed; study data saves locally.

Single workspace

Library, flashcards, quiz, and chat stay in one flow.

Clear study priorities

Weak-topic tracking shows what to review next.

Simple to present

The architecture is lightweight and demo-friendly.

Why usability matters here

Students often stop using tools that feel complicated, even if the features are strong. This design focuses on predictable flows, low setup cost, and clear next actions so the app is actually used repeatedly before quizzes and tests.

Navigation and interaction choices

The app keeps related actions close together. Students can move from note library to flashcards, then to quiz, without losing context. This reduces tab-switching and helps maintain focus during short study windows.

Local-first tradeoffs

Storing progress in local browser storage removes account friction and keeps setup fast. The tradeoff is that data is tied to a device/browser profile unless exported. For a class project and everyday personal studying, this is often a good balance between simplicity and usefulness.

How this helps real study behavior

Instead of a generic dashboard, the interface points students toward concrete actions: review cards, run a quiz, and revisit weak topics. That makes it easier to turn study time into specific progress rather than passive reading.
